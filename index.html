asexual

[a-seks-wal]

noun, plural a.seks.wals.

1. Someone who doesn't experience sexual attraction.

2. Not a phase, celibacy, abstinence, etc.

Did you know:

Approximately 1% of the population identifies as asexual. That means there are more than 7.7 million aces in the world!

Asexuality is...

A valid identity worthy of love and acceptance. End of story.

Asexuals can...

  • Fall in love
  • Enjoy sex
  • Experience arousal
  • Be anyone
  • Have or want kids
  • Want friendship

Asexuality isn't...

  • Absitnence or celibacy
  • Sexual repression
  • Loss of libido
  • Fear of intimacy
  • Sexual aversion
  • Inability to find a partner
  • A low sex drive
  • A hormone imbalance
  • Another name for HSDD
  • A mental illness
  • Going against evolution
  • Caused by immaturity

What is asexuality?

Asexuality is an umbrella term for people who do not experience sexual attraction. The term "ace" is often used as shorthand for someone who identifies as asexual. Many identities fall under this umbrella.

Representation

Seeing people who look like you is important with any group of people. It helps to solidify your sense of self.

Ace culture often gets hidden by both mainstream LGBTQIA+ and cis-hetero cultures. Representation of aces often gets tied to aliens or people who are deemed as “fixable.”

Resources

There are several different websites that provide information about asexuality and figuring out who you are and how you identify on the spectrum.

They’re helpful for general questions and definitions, and often provide sources for non-aces, too.
